Research focus

Martian nightside plasma

Mars has no global intrinsic magnetic field. Its nightside plasma environment is shaped by solar wind interaction, crustal fields, dayside-to-nightside transport, and energetic electron precipitation.

01

Nightside ionosphere

Global distributions of nightside ionospheric detections and peak electron densities observed by Mars Express/MARSIS.

02

Auroral coupling

Testing whether auroral electron precipitation explains where and when dense nightside plasma appears.

03

Ion composition

Using MAVEN/NGIMS to separate transport, ion chemistry, and particle-impact ionization on the nightside.

Recent paper

Reinvestigating the nightside ionosphere of Mars

Eight years of Mars Express and MAVEN data reveal how the nightside ionosphere responds to solar wind conditions, IMF geometry, and crustal magnetic fields.

  • Weak crustal-field regions show enhanced detection under favorable solar wind conditions.
  • Strong crustal-field regions host localized high-density structures.
  • The next step is to test source mechanisms using ion composition and auroral observations.
