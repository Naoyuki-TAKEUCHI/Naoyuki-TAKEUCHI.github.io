Research

Martian nightside plasma, from electrons to ions.

My work explores how plasma is produced and structured on the nightside of Mars, where sunlight is absent but solar-wind interaction, crustal magnetic fields, and energetic particles still matter.

Theme 01

Global nightside ionosphere observed by MARSIS

Mars Express/MARSIS active ionospheric sounding provides a unique way to identify nightside ionospheric traces and estimate peak electron densities. I use long-term MARSIS observations to investigate where the nightside ionosphere appears, how dense it becomes, and how its distribution varies across solar wind and magnetic environments.

Theme 02

Solar wind control and crustal magnetic fields

The Martian nightside ionosphere is shaped by upstream solar wind drivers and regional crustal magnetic fields. I examine the roles of solar wind dynamic pressure, interplanetary magnetic field orientation, convective electric-field hemispheres, and crustal-field strength in controlling nightside plasma formation.

Theme 03

Ion composition from MAVEN/NGIMS

MAVEN/NGIMS measurements allow the nightside ionosphere to be studied by ion species. I am interested in how O⁺, O₂⁺, CO₂⁺, NO⁺ and other ions vary with altitude, solar zenith angle, local time, magnetic topology, and upstream conditions.

Theme 04

Aurora–ionosphere coupling

Electron precipitation can produce aurora and enhance ionization on the nightside. A next step is to compare MARSIS nightside ionosphere detections with auroral electron observations and ultraviolet aurora to test the production mechanisms more directly.

Research statement draft

I aim to understand how the Martian nightside ionosphere is generated and organized under the influence of solar wind interaction, crustal magnetic fields, and auroral electron precipitation. By combining Mars Express and MAVEN observations, my research connects electron density structures, ion composition, and particle precipitation to build a more complete picture of plasma processes around Mars.
